NRZ vs RZ feedback DAC — tier-1 CT ΣΔ modulator

1st order, 1-bit, fs = 50 MHz, input 0.3 V (-4.4 dBFS) at 27.5 kHz, 16384 bits per run. Feedback charge per period is equalized (NRZ uses 2×RDAC). "Asymmetric" raises the pull-down switch resistance 100 → 2000 Ω against a 100 fF DAC-node capacitance — about 190 ps of rise/fall asymmetry, the ISI mechanism.

Caveat on absolute numbers: 1st-order in-band noise is dominated by pattern tones; at a 16k window the ideal tier-0 model scatters 53.6–66.8 dB across dither seeds (long-run converges to ~66 dB, ~10 ENOB). All four variants here share one fixed noise seed (rndseed=17), so the RZ/NRZ and sym/asym differences are paired and meaningful even though each absolute value carries the window-luck spread.
